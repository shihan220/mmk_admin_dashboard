TAX STRATEGIES

Essential tax planning strategies for individuals and businesses.

01 — STRATEGIES

Effective Tax Planning

01

Salary vs Dividends

For director-shareholders of limited companies, the split between salary and dividends is one of the most effective ways to minimise your overall tax burden. The optimal strategy for 2025/26 typically involves drawing a salary of £12,570 (the Personal Allowance) to preserve your State Pension entitlement and NI record, then extracting remaining profits as dividends. Dividends are not subject to National Insurance, making them more tax-efficient than salary above the Personal Allowance. However, each situation is unique — factors such as other income sources, employment allowance availability, and pension contributions all affect the optimal split. We recommend reviewing your remuneration strategy annually.

02

Pension Contributions

Pension contributions remain one of the most powerful tax planning tools available. Employer pension contributions are fully deductible against Corporation Tax and are not subject to National Insurance for either the employer or employee, making them doubly efficient. Individuals can contribute up to £60,000 per year (the Annual Allowance) and receive tax relief at their marginal rate. Higher and additional rate taxpayers receive 40% or 45% relief respectively, making pension contributions particularly valuable. You can also carry forward unused allowance from the previous three tax years, potentially contributing up to £240,000 in a single year. Salary sacrifice arrangements can provide additional NI savings for both employer and employee.

03

Capital Allowances

Capital allowances provide tax relief on qualifying capital expenditure. The Annual Investment Allowance (AIA) allows businesses to deduct up to £1,000,000 of qualifying plant and machinery expenditure in the year of purchase — providing an immediate 100% deduction against taxable profits. Full Expensing at 100% is permanently available for companies investing in qualifying new plant and machinery, with a 50% First Year Allowance for special rate assets. Structures and Buildings Allowance (SBA) provides 3% per annum relief on qualifying commercial construction costs. Careful timing of capital expenditure can make a significant difference to your tax bill, particularly around year-end.

04

R&D Tax Credits

Research and Development tax credits reward companies that invest in innovation. Under the merged R&D scheme from April 2024, qualifying companies can claim an above-the-line credit of 20% on qualifying R&D expenditure. R&D-intensive SMEs (where qualifying R&D spend represents 30% or more of total expenditure) may qualify for Enhanced R&D Intensive Support with a higher rate of relief. Qualifying activities include developing new products, processes or services, or appreciably improving existing ones. Common qualifying costs include staff costs, subcontractor payments, software, consumables and cloud computing. Many businesses undertake qualifying R&D without realising it — if your business solves technical problems or develops new solutions, you may be eligible to claim.

05

ISA & Investment Planning

Individual Savings Accounts (ISAs) offer a tax-free wrapper for your savings and investments. The annual ISA allowance is £20,000, which can be split across Cash ISAs, Stocks and Shares ISAs, Innovative Finance ISAs, and Lifetime ISAs. All income and capital gains within an ISA are completely free of UK tax, making them an essential part of any tax planning strategy. For longer-term investments, a Stocks and Shares ISA can be particularly powerful as it shelters dividend income and capital gains from tax indefinitely. Couples can effectively shelter £40,000 per year between them. When combined with pension contributions, ISAs form the cornerstone of a tax-efficient savings and investment strategy.

06

Incorporation

For sole traders and partnerships with profits above approximately £50,000, incorporating as a limited company can deliver significant tax savings. A limited company pays Corporation Tax at 19% on profits up to £50,000 (compared to income tax rates of up to 45%), and profits can be extracted tax-efficiently through a combination of salary and dividends. Incorporation also provides limited liability protection, separating your personal assets from business debts. However, incorporation is not always beneficial — the administrative burden increases, and there are costs associated with running a limited company including statutory accounts, Corporation Tax returns and Companies House filings. We can model your specific circumstances to determine whether incorporation would save you money.
