Engagement Terms

Our engagement with you will be set out in a separate letter of engagement, which will detail the specific services we will provide, the responsibilities of both parties, and the agreed fees. These Terms should be read in conjunction with your letter of engagement.

Where there is any conflict between these Terms and your letter of engagement, the letter of engagement shall take precedence. We reserve the right to amend these Terms from time to time, and any material changes will be communicated to you in writing.

Our engagement commences upon your acceptance of our letter of engagement, whether by signing and returning it or by providing us with instructions to proceed with the agreed services.

Our Services

We provide a range of professional accountancy and advisory services, which may include but are not limited to:

  • Preparation of annual statutory accounts and management accounts
  • Personal and corporate tax planning and advisory
  • Self-assessment tax return preparation and filing
  • Corporation tax return preparation and filing
  • VAT registration, returns, and advisory
  • Bookkeeping and management accounting
  • Payroll processing and Real Time Information (RTI) submissions
  • Company formation and company secretarial services
  • Business start-up advisory
  • Tax investigation and HMRC enquiry support
  • Cloud accounting setup and support

The specific services to be provided will be detailed in your letter of engagement. We will carry out our work with reasonable skill and care, and in accordance with the applicable professional standards and ethical guidelines issued by the Association of Chartered Certified Accountants (ACCA).

Our services are based on the information you provide to us. We do not accept responsibility for errors or omissions arising from incorrect or incomplete information provided by the Client.

Fees and Payment

Our fees will be set out in your letter of engagement. Unless otherwise agreed in writing, the following terms apply:

  • Fixed fees: Where we agree a fixed fee, this covers the specific scope of work set out in your letter of engagement. Additional work outside this scope will be charged separately and agreed with you in advance
  • Time-based fees: Where work is charged on a time basis, we will provide you with an estimate of fees where possible. Hourly rates vary depending on the seniority and specialism of the staff involved
  • Disbursements: Expenses incurred on your behalf (such as Companies House filing fees, software subscriptions, or travel costs) will be recharged at cost
  • Payment terms: Invoices are payable within 14 days of the date of issue, unless otherwise agreed in writing
  • Late payment: We reserve the right to charge interest on overdue invoices at the rate of 4% above the Bank of England base rate, in accordance with the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998

We may require payment on account for certain services or new client engagements. Where an engagement is terminated prior to completion, we will invoice for all work completed to date and any disbursements incurred.

All fees quoted are exclusive of VAT, which will be charged at the prevailing rate where applicable.

Client Obligations

  • Notify us promptly of any changes to your circumstances that may affect our work or the advice we provide
  • Respond to our queries and requests for information within a reasonable timeframe
  • Keep proper records and books of account as required by law
  • Provide us with reasonable notice of any deadlines we need to be aware of

  • Pay our fees in accordance with the agreed payment terms

You acknowledge that we cannot be held responsible for any penalties, interest, or surcharges levied by HMRC or other bodies where these arise from late or inaccurate information provided by you, or from your failure to act upon our advice in a timely manner.

Confidentiality

We treat all client information as confidential. We will not disclose your information to any third party without your prior consent, except where:

  • We are required to do so by law, regulation, or by order of a court or regulatory authority
  • Disclosure is necessary for the proper performance of our services (for example, communicating with HMRC on your behalf)
  • Disclosure is required by our professional body (ACCA) for quality assurance or regulatory purposes
  • We are required to make a disclosure under the anti-money laundering regulations

Our duty of confidentiality continues beyond the termination of our engagement with you. Please refer to our Privacy Policy for full details of how we handle your personal data.

Complaints Procedure

We are committed to providing a high standard of service. If you are dissatisfied with any aspect of our work, we encourage you to raise it with us promptly so that we can address your concerns.

  1. Initial complaint: Please raise your complaint in writing to your engagement manager or to the firm’s managing director at mail@mmkaccountants.com
  2. Acknowledgement: We will acknowledge receipt of your complaint within five working days
  3. Investigation: We will investigate your complaint thoroughly and provide a full written response within 28 days of receipt. If we require additional time, we will notify you accordingly

MMK Accountants Ltd is regulated by the Association of Chartered Certified Accountants (ACCA). We are bound by and comply with the following:

  • ACCA’s Code of Ethics and Conduct, which is based on the International Ethics Standards Board for Accountants (IESBA) Code
  • The fundamental principles of integrity, objectivity, professional competence and due care, confidentiality, and professional behaviour
  • ACCA’s Global Practising Regulations and associated guidelines
  • All applicable UK legislation, including the Companies Act 2006, the Taxes Management Act 1970, and the Money Laundering Regulations 2017
  • International Standards on Auditing (UK) where applicable

We maintain our professional competence through ongoing continuing professional development (CPD) and are subject to periodic monitoring visits by ACCA to ensure compliance with applicable standards.

Termination

Either party may terminate the engagement by giving written notice to the other party. The following terms apply upon termination:

  • We will be entitled to payment for all work completed up to the date of termination, including any disbursements incurred
  • We will return all original documents and records provided by you, subject to the exercise of any lien for unpaid fees
  • We retain the right to retain copies of working papers created during the engagement
  • We will cooperate with any successor advisers to ensure a smooth transition, subject to the payment of any outstanding fees
  • Our obligations of confidentiality will continue to apply following termination

We reserve the right to terminate our engagement immediately if we discover that you have provided us with misleading or inaccurate information, if continuing the engagement would conflict with our professional obligations, or if there has been a fundamental breakdown in the relationship of trust and confidence between us.
