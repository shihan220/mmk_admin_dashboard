01 — PERSONAL FINANCE

Managing Your Finances

Self-Assessment Guide

If you are self-employed, a company director, a landlord, or have untaxed income, you will need to complete a Self-Assessment tax return each year. Understanding the key deadlines is essential to avoid penalties.

  • Register for Self-Assessment by 5 October following the end of the tax year in which you first need to file
  • Paper tax return deadline: 31 October following the end of the tax year
  • Online tax return deadline: 31 January following the end of the tax year
  • Payment on account — two advance payments due on 31 January and 31 July
  • Allowable expenses you can claim to reduce your tax bill
  • Late filing penalty of £100 applies immediately after the deadline

Property & Landlord Tax

The UK property tax landscape has changed significantly in recent years. Landlords face restrictions on mortgage interest relief, higher CGT rates, and new reporting requirements.

  • Section 24 — mortgage interest relief restricted to basic rate (20%) tax credit for individual landlords
  • Capital Gains Tax on residential property: 18% (basic rate) or 24% (higher rate)
  • 60-day CGT reporting requirement for UK residential property disposals
  • Stamp Duty Land Tax surcharge of 5% on additional residential properties
  • Furnished Holiday Let (FHL) regime abolished from April 2025
  • Incorporating a property portfolio — benefits and considerations

Pensions & Retirement

Pension planning is one of the most tax-efficient ways to save for your future. Understanding the allowances, tax relief available, and your options at retirement is crucial for maximising your pension pot.

  • Annual Allowance of £60,000 — the maximum you can contribute and receive tax relief each year
  • Tax relief at your marginal rate — basic rate taxpayers get 20%, higher rate 40%, additional rate 45%
  • 25% tax-free lump sum available when you access your pension (up to £268,275)
  • Carry forward unused allowance from the previous three tax years
  • Lifetime Allowance abolished from April 2024 — but lump sum limits remain
  • Salary sacrifice pension arrangements — saving both income tax and National Insurance

Inheritance Tax Planning

Inheritance Tax (IHT) is charged at 40% on estates above the nil-rate band. With proper planning, it is possible to significantly reduce or even eliminate the IHT liability on your estate.

  • Nil-Rate Band (NRB) of £325,000 — the threshold below which no IHT is payable
  • Residence Nil-Rate Band (RNRB) of £175,000 when passing a family home to direct descendants
  • Combined allowance of up to £1,000,000 for married couples and civil partners
  • IHT is charged at 40% on the value of the estate above the available thresholds
  • Annual gift exemption of £3,000 per year plus small gifts of £250 per recipient
  • Business Property Relief (BPR) and Agricultural Property Relief (APR) — potentially 100% relief
