EXPATRIATE & NON-DOMICILED TAX

Expert international tax advisory for individuals arriving in, departing from, or with cross-border financial interests in the United Kingdom.

01 — OVERVIEW

International tax planning for a globally connected world

The UK's tax rules for expatriates and non-domiciled individuals are among the most complex in the world. The Statutory Residence Test (SRT), the remittance basis of taxation, deemed domicile rules, and an extensive network of double taxation agreements all interact to determine your UK tax liability — and getting it wrong can be extremely costly.

Whether you are a foreign national arriving in the UK for work, a British expatriate returning after years abroad, or a long-term resident with overseas income and assets, your tax position requires careful analysis. Key considerations include split-year treatment, the remittance basis charge, overseas workday relief, the taxation of overseas trusts and structures, and inheritance tax exposure on worldwide assets.

MMK Accountants' expatriate tax team advises individuals from over 40 countries. We combine deep technical knowledge of UK residence and domicile rules with a practical understanding of how international tax systems interact. Our aim is to ensure you are fully compliant in the UK while legitimately minimising your overall tax burden across jurisdictions.

02 — HOW WE HELP
01/ Tax Residency Status

Determining your UK tax residence status under the Statutory Residence Test (SRT) is the essential first step. The SRT involves automatic tests, sufficient ties tests, and day-counting rules that must be applied with precision. We carry out a full SRT analysis, advise on split-year treatment where applicable, and help you maintain proper records of your UK and overseas days to support your claimed status.

  • Full Statutory Residence Test (SRT) analysis
  • Day-counting records and compliance systems
  • Split-year treatment eligibility assessment
  • Temporary non-residence anti-avoidance advice
02/ Remittance Basis Claims

Non-domiciled individuals may be able to claim the remittance basis, paying UK tax only on foreign income and gains that are brought into (remitted to) the UK. However, the rules are intricate — the annual remittance basis charge, mixed fund rules, and the definition of what constitutes a remittance all require careful management. We advise on whether the remittance basis is beneficial, structure overseas accounts to avoid inadvertent remittances, and prepare the necessary HMRC claims.

  • Remittance basis cost-benefit analysis
  • Annual remittance basis charge calculations (£30,000/£60,000)
  • Mixed fund cleansing and segregation strategies
  • Nominated income elections and compliance
03/ Double Taxation Relief

When income or gains are taxable in more than one country, double taxation agreements (DTAs) and unilateral relief provisions can prevent you from paying tax twice on the same income. We identify the applicable treaty provisions, calculate relief claims accurately, and ensure your UK tax return correctly reflects overseas tax paid. We also advise on treaty residence tie-breaker provisions where dual residence arises.

  • Double taxation agreement analysis and treaty claims
  • Foreign tax credit relief calculations
  • Treaty residence tie-breaker applications
  • Overseas pension and employment income treaty relief
04/ Arrival & Departure Planning

The year of arrival in or departure from the UK presents both opportunities and risks. Pre-arrival planning can help structure overseas assets and income before UK tax obligations arise, while departure planning ensures you do not trigger unexpected capital gains tax charges or leave ongoing UK tax liabilities unaddressed. We provide comprehensive checklists and strategic advice for both scenarios.

  • Pre-arrival tax structuring and asset review
  • Departure checklist and UK ongoing obligations
  • Capital gains tax departure planning
  • Overseas workday relief claims for new arrivals
