PROPERTY TAX SPECIALISTS

Comprehensive tax planning and compliance services for landlords, property investors and developers across residential and commercial portfolios.

01 — OVERVIEW

Maximise returns and minimise tax on your property portfolio

UK property taxation has undergone significant reform in recent years. The restriction of mortgage interest relief under Section 24, higher rates of Stamp Duty Land Tax (SDLT) for additional dwellings, changes to Capital Gains Tax (CGT) reporting timelines, and the introduction of the 60-day CGT reporting requirement have all increased the complexity and cost of property ownership.

Whether you own a single buy-to-let property or manage a large portfolio of residential and commercial assets, understanding the tax implications at every stage — acquisition, ownership, disposal and succession — is essential. Poor planning can erode rental profits, trigger unexpected SDLT surcharges, or result in avoidable capital gains tax on disposal.

MMK Accountants' property tax team advises individual landlords, property partnerships, limited company SPVs, and property developers. We provide strategic advice on structuring property ownership, ongoing compliance with rental income reporting, and proactive planning for disposals — ensuring you retain as much of your property income and gains as the law permits.

02 — HOW WE HELP
01/ Buy-to-Let Accounting

We manage the complete accounting and tax compliance for buy-to-let landlords, from recording rental income and allowable expenses to preparing property pages on your Self Assessment return. We ensure all legitimate deductions are claimed — including repairs, letting agent fees, insurance, and the basic rate tax reduction for finance costs — and advise on the most efficient ownership structure for your circumstances.

  • Rental income and expense reporting on Self Assessment
  • Allowable expense identification and record-keeping
  • Finance cost restriction (Section 24) calculations
  • Furnished holiday lettings (FHL) qualification and tax treatment
02/ SDLT Planning

Stamp Duty Land Tax can represent a significant cost on property acquisitions, particularly with the 3% surcharge on additional residential properties and the 2% surcharge for non-UK residents. We advise on SDLT reliefs and exemptions — including Multiple Dwellings Relief, mixed-use property claims, and first-time buyer relief — and ensure your SDLT return is filed accurately within the 14-day deadline.

  • SDLT calculations including surcharge assessments
  • Multiple Dwellings Relief (MDR) claims
  • Mixed-use property classification and reduced rates
  • SDLT return preparation and 14-day filing compliance
03/ Capital Gains on Property

The disposal of UK residential property by individuals now requires a CGT return to HMRC within 60 days, with payment of any tax due at the same time. We calculate your capital gain accurately — applying principal private residence relief, lettings relief, and annual exempt amounts where applicable — prepare the 60-day return, and reconcile the position on your annual Self Assessment.

  • 60-day UK residential property CGT return filing
  • Principal private residence relief and lettings relief claims
  • Capital gains computation including enhancement expenditure
  • Annual exempt amount optimisation and spouse transfers
04/ Section 24 & Incorporation

The restriction of mortgage interest relief to a basic rate tax credit under Section 24 has significantly increased the effective tax rate for higher-rate taxpaying landlords. We model the financial impact of incorporating your property portfolio into a limited company SPV, considering SDLT costs, CGT on transfer, ongoing Corporation Tax rates, and profit extraction — giving you a clear picture of whether incorporation is beneficial for your specific situation.

  • Section 24 impact modelling and tax projections
  • Incorporation cost-benefit analysis (SDLT, CGT, Corporation Tax)
  • HMRC incorporation relief claims under s162 TCGA 1992
  • SPV company setup, ongoing accounts and compliance
