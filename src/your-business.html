01 — BUSINESS GUIDES

Running Your Business Successfully

Starting a Business

Setting up a new business involves important decisions about structure, registration and compliance. Getting it right from the start saves time, money and stress further down the line.

  • Choosing the right business structure — sole trader, partnership, or limited company
  • Registering with Companies House and obtaining your Certificate of Incorporation
  • Registering for Corporation Tax, PAYE and VAT with HMRC
  • Opening a dedicated business bank account
  • Understanding your statutory filing obligations and deadlines
  • Setting up proper bookkeeping systems from day one

Understanding VAT

Value Added Tax is one of the most complex areas of UK taxation. Understanding when to register, which scheme suits your business, and how to stay compliant is essential.

  • Mandatory VAT registration when taxable turnover exceeds £90,000 in any 12-month period
  • Choosing the right VAT scheme — Standard, Flat Rate, Cash Accounting, or Annual Accounting
  • Making Tax Digital (MTD) for VAT — mandatory digital record-keeping and filing
  • Submitting accurate quarterly VAT returns to HMRC
  • Understanding partial exemption for businesses making exempt supplies
  • Voluntary registration — when it makes sense to register below the threshold

Employing Staff

Taking on employees brings significant responsibilities. From PAYE registration to pension auto-enrolment, employers must comply with a range of legal and tax obligations.

  • Registering as an employer with HMRC and setting up PAYE
  • Auto-enrolment pension obligations — minimum contributions currently 8% (3% employer, 5% employee)
  • Real Time Information (RTI) — reporting payroll to HMRC on or before each pay date
  • Employment Allowance of £10,500 to reduce your employer NI bill
  • Understanding employment contracts, statutory sick pay, and holiday entitlements
  • Issuing P45s, P60s and payslips correctly

Making Tax Digital

HMRC's Making Tax Digital programme is transforming how businesses keep records and submit tax returns. Understanding the timeline and requirements is critical for compliance.

  • MTD for VAT is now mandatory for all VAT-registered businesses
  • Digital records must be maintained using MTD-compatible software
  • MTD for Income Tax Self-Assessment (MTD for ITSA) starts April 2026 for incomes over £50,000
  • MTD for ITSA extends to incomes over £30,000 from April 2027
  • Quarterly reporting will replace the annual Self-Assessment return
  • Choosing the right MTD-compatible software for your business

Annual Accounts & Filing

Every limited company must prepare and file annual accounts with Companies House and a Corporation Tax return with HMRC. Missing deadlines results in automatic penalties.

  • Annual accounts must be filed with Companies House within 9 months of the accounting year-end
  • Corporation Tax return (CT600) must be filed within 12 months of the accounting period end
  • Corporation Tax payment is due 9 months and 1 day after the accounting period end
  • Confirmation Statement must be filed at least once every 12 months
  • Late filing penalties range from £150 to £1,500 for Companies House
  • iXBRL tagging requirements for Corporation Tax computations and accounts

HMRC Tax Investigations

An HMRC investigation can be stressful and time-consuming. Understanding the different types of enquiry and your rights can help you navigate the process with confidence.

  • Compliance checks — routine reviews of specific aspects of your tax return
  • Full investigations — comprehensive reviews of all your business records and personal finances
  • Aspect enquiries — HMRC questions about a particular entry on your return
  • Your rights during an investigation, including the right to professional representation
  • How to respond to an HMRC enquiry letter
  • Fee Protection Insurance — covering professional fees during an investigation
