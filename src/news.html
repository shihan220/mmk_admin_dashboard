Making Tax DigitalFebruary 2026

Making Tax Digital for Income Tax: What You Need to Know Before April 2026

From April 2026, sole traders and landlords with gross income over £50,000 will be required to keep digital records and submit quarterly updates to HMRC through MTD-compatible software. This is the biggest change to the self-assessment system in a generation.

Who is affected?

  • Sole traders with gross self-employment income over £50,000
  • Landlords with gross property income over £50,000
  • From April 2027, the threshold drops to £30,000

What do you need to do?

  • Sign up for MTD for Income Tax through your Government Gateway account
  • Choose MTD-compatible software (e.g. Xero, FreeAgent, QuickBooks)
  • Keep digital records of all income and expenses
  • Submit quarterly updates to HMRC (within 1 month of each quarter end)
  • Submit a final declaration instead of a self-assessment return

Tax UpdateFebruary 2026

Self-Assessment Deadline: What to Do If You Missed 31 January

Over 1 million taxpayers missed the 31 January self-assessment deadline. If you're one of them, don't panic — but act quickly. HMRC applies an immediate £100 late filing penalty, with further penalties accumulating after 3, 6 and 12 months. Interest is also charged on any unpaid tax from the due date.

Key actions: File as soon as possible, pay any tax owed, consider setting up a Time to Pay arrangement with HMRC, and contact us if you need help.
Business NewsJanuary 2026

Employer NI Increase: How to Prepare Your Business

From April 2025, the employer NI rate increased to 13.8% and the secondary threshold was reduced to £5,000 (from £9,100). The Employment Allowance increased to £10,500 and is now available to all eligible employers regardless of previous year's NI liability.

Impact: Most employers will see increased NI costs. Review your payroll strategy and consider salary sacrifice arrangements for pension contributions to mitigate the impact.
Budget UpdateJanuary 2026

Autumn Budget 2025: Key Changes for Businesses and Individuals

The Chancellor's Autumn Budget brought several significant changes including increases to Capital Gains Tax rates (now 18% and 24%), changes to Business Asset Disposal Relief (rising to 14%, then 18% in 2026), and the introduction of VAT on private school fees.

Key changes: CGT rates aligned at 18%/24%, employer NI up to 13.8%, Inheritance Tax on pensions from April 2027, Agricultural and Business Property Relief reformed.
Hot TopicDecember 2025

Non-Dom Tax Changes: End of the Remittance Basis

From April 2025, the remittance basis of taxation for non-UK domiciled individuals has been abolished and replaced with a new 4-year Foreign Income and Gains (FIG) regime. Existing non-doms who have been UK tax resident for less than 4 years can elect into the new regime.

Action needed: Non-dom clients should review their tax position urgently. The Temporary Repatriation Facility allows previously unremitted foreign income to be brought to the UK at reduced rates.
Business NewsNovember 2025

Electric Vehicle Benefits: Company Car Tax Rates 2025/26

Electric vehicles remain highly tax-efficient for company car drivers. The Benefit-in-Kind rate for pure electric vehicles is just 2% for 2025/26, rising to 3% in 2026/27 and 4% in 2027/28. This makes EVs significantly cheaper than petrol or diesel alternatives through a company.

Savings: A £40,000 EV has a BIK of just £800 (taxed at £160 for basic rate taxpayer vs potentially £3,000+ for equivalent ICE vehicle). Employer saves NI too.
Tax UpdateOctober 2025

Corporation Tax: Marginal Relief Explained

Companies with profits between £50,000 and £250,000 benefit from marginal relief, resulting in an effective tax rate between 19% and 25%. Understanding how this works is crucial for tax planning, especially around year-end when timing of income and expenses can impact the rate.

Planning tip: If profits are near £50,000, consider timing of expenses, pension contributions, and capital allowances to stay within the small profits rate.
