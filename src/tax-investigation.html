TAX INVESTIGATION SUPPORT

Professional representation and expert defence when you face an HMRC enquiry, compliance check or tax investigation of any kind.

01 — OVERVIEW

Expert representation when HMRC comes calling

Receiving a letter from HMRC opening an enquiry into your tax affairs can be one of the most stressful experiences for any individual or business owner. Whether it is a routine compliance check into a single aspect of your return, a full enquiry into your entire tax position, or a serious investigation under Code of Practice 8 or 9, how you respond in the early stages is critical to the outcome.

HMRC has significantly increased its compliance activity and investment in data analytics in recent years. Connect, their risk assessment system, cross-references information from banks, land registry, Companies House, overseas tax authorities and third-party data sources to identify discrepancies and potential underpayments. Random enquiries also remain a possibility. The reality is that any taxpayer can find themselves under investigation, regardless of whether they have done anything wrong.

MMK Accountants' tax investigation team has extensive experience defending clients against HMRC enquiries at all levels — from straightforward aspect enquiries to complex fraud investigations. We act as your representative, handling all correspondence with HMRC, attending meetings on your behalf, negotiating settlements, and working to minimise penalties. Our priority is to resolve the matter as quickly and cost-effectively as possible while protecting your interests at every stage.

02 — HOW WE HELP
01/ HMRC Enquiry Representation

We represent you throughout the entire HMRC enquiry process, from the initial opening letter to final settlement or closure. We review HMRC's information requests critically, prepare and submit responses on your behalf, attend meetings with HMRC officers alongside you or in your absence, and challenge unreasonable demands for information. Our aim is to contain the scope of the enquiry and achieve the best possible outcome for you.

  • Full representation for Section 9A and Schedule 36 enquiries
  • Information request review and strategic response preparation
  • Attendance at HMRC meetings and interviews
  • Aspect enquiry and full enquiry management
02/ Voluntary Disclosures

If you have undeclared income, unreported capital gains, or errors in previous tax returns, making a voluntary disclosure to HMRC before they discover the issue will almost always result in significantly lower penalties than if HMRC finds the error themselves. We manage voluntary disclosures through the appropriate HMRC facility — whether the Worldwide Disclosure Facility, Let Property Income Campaign, or a direct approach to your tax office — ensuring the disclosure is complete, accurate and presented in the most favourable light.

  • Worldwide Disclosure Facility (WDF) submissions
  • Let Property Income Campaign disclosures
  • Contractual Disclosure Facility (CDF) — Code of Practice 9 responses
  • Error quantification and tax liability calculations
03/ Penalty Negotiation

HMRC penalties for inaccuracies, late filing, late payment and failure to notify can be substantial — up to 100% of the tax due in some cases, or 200% for offshore matters. However, penalties can be significantly reduced based on the quality of your disclosure, the level of cooperation shown, and whether the error was careless, deliberate, or deliberate and concealed. We negotiate aggressively with HMRC to achieve the maximum penalty reduction by demonstrating the quality of your disclosure under the "telling, helping, giving" framework.

  • Penalty suspension applications for careless errors
  • "Telling, helping, giving" quality of disclosure maximisation
  • Reasonable excuse and special reduction arguments
  • Time to pay arrangements for outstanding liabilities
04/ Code of Practice 9

Code of Practice 9 (COP9) is HMRC's most serious civil investigation procedure, reserved for cases where HMRC suspects tax fraud. A COP9 investigation offers the Contractual Disclosure Facility (CDF) as an alternative to criminal prosecution, but it requires a complete and truthful disclosure of all deliberate conduct. This is an extremely high-stakes process that demands experienced, specialist representation. We guide clients through every stage of COP9, from the initial decision on whether to accept the CDF offer to the preparation of the Outline Disclosure and Formal Disclosure documents.

  • COP9 and Contractual Disclosure Facility (CDF) advice
  • Outline Disclosure and Formal Disclosure preparation
  • Liaison with HMRC's Fraud Investigation Service (FIS)
  • Settlement negotiation and payment arrangements
