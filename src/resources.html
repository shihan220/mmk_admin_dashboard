05 — TAX STRATEGIES

Essential Tax Planning Strategies

01

Salary vs Dividends

For director-shareholders, the optimal mix of salary and dividends can save thousands. A salary of £12,570 (to preserve NI record) combined with dividends is typically the most tax-efficient approach for 2025/26.

02

Pension Contributions

Employer pension contributions are a Corporation Tax deduction and not subject to NI. Consider making company contributions of up to £60,000 annually, or more using carry-forward rules from the previous 3 years.

03

Capital Allowances

The Annual Investment Allowance (AIA) provides 100% first-year relief on qualifying plant and machinery up to £1,000,000. Full Expensing allows 100% relief on qualifying new plant and machinery with no cap.

04

R&D Tax Credits

If your company carries out qualifying research and development activities, you may be able to claim the merged R&D scheme providing a 20% above-the-line credit on qualifying expenditure.

05

ISA & Investment Planning

Use your annual ISA allowance (£20,000) to shelter investment returns from tax. Consider combining Cash ISAs, Stocks & Shares ISAs, and the new British ISA for maximum tax-free savings.

06

Incorporation

Sole traders and partnerships with profits above c.£50,000 may benefit from incorporation as a limited company. The lower corporation tax rate (19-25%) combined with dividend extraction can provide significant savings.
